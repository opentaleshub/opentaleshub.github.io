About this Story

"The Tell-Tale Heart" is a short story by American writer Edgar Allan Poe, first published in 1843. It is told by an unnamed narrator who insists on his sanity after murdering an old man with a "vulture eye". The story is a classic example of Gothic horror and psychological thriller, exploring themes of guilt, paranoia, and the unreliability of the human mind. The narrator's descent into madness and his obsession with the old man's eye creates an atmosphere of intense psychological tension that has made this one of Poe's most famous and enduring works.
